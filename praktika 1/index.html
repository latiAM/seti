Biography

American basketball player Kobe Bean Bryant was born August 23, 1978 in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, the son of former NBA player Joe Bryant. He spent his childhood in Italy, where his father worked as a coach. He graduated high school in 1996 and was drafted by the NBA. Picked in the first round with the 13th overall pick by the Charlotte Hornets. On 26 January 2020, Bryant died in a helicopter crash in California.

NBA

He made his NBA debut on 3 November 1996. He became the youngest player to appear in the starting line-up in an NBA game, 18 years 2 months and 11 days old at the time of the game. In 1998 he became the youngest player to start in the NBA All-Star Game. In December 2015, he announced his retirement from the NBA. In April 2016, he played his final game in which he scored 60 points.

Achievements

He was NBA champion in 2000, 2001, 2002, 2009 and 2010. Won the 2007 US National Team USA Basketball Championship. Olympic champion in 2008 and 2012. Winner of the Teens Choice Award (2001, 2002). In 2017, he produced and wrote the animated short film Dear Basketball, which won the Academy Award for Best Animated Short Film (2018).
